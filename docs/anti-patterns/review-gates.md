# Review Gates

Use these gates before accepting writing or design work.

## Universal Gate

Reject if:

- the artifact has no named audience
- the artifact has no single decision to unblock
- the artifact adds more than it removes
- the artifact uses labels to compensate for weak structure
- the artifact relies on template sections
- the artifact claims quality without screenshots, sources, or evidence

## Copy Gate

Reject if:

- the heading needs an eyebrow label to make sense
- the intro defines obvious terms
- the copy uses hype words without evidence
- the CTA is generic
- the page says "learn more" when it can name the destination
- the copy explains why to care instead of making the value obvious
- the copy uses "not just X, but Y"

Pass only if:

- every heading can stand alone
- every sentence has a job
- every claim has a proof path
- every CTA points to a real next action

## Design Gate

Reject if:

- the page is mostly text
- the page has no real artifact
- the page uses cards for product taxonomy
- the page has tiny labels above headings
- the page has decorative chips
- screenshots are missing
- desktop and mobile were not checked
- changed files were not grepped for banned label patterns

Pass only if:

- the first viewport makes one decision clear
- the visual system is visible without reading every word
- product proof is real
- hierarchy survives if labels are removed

## Blog Gate

Reject if:

- the post is SEO filler
- the first section defines an obvious term
- comparison posts have no criteria or tradeoffs
- internal links exist only for SEO
- the post ends with a generic CTA
- the post is labeled research without research standards

Pass only if:

- the reader learns something specific
- the post names a decision, failure mode, or implementation detail
- sources are current when the topic changes over time
- tradeoffs are explicit

## Research Gate

Reject if:

- it is a product comparison
- it is a market explainer
- it has no thesis
- it has no method, evidence, or model
- it has a marketing CTA
- it uses dark landing-page styling

Pass only if:

- it makes a narrow claim
- it can be argued against
- evidence or assumptions are visible
- limitations are named

## AI Product Gate

Reject if:

- AI output appears verified when it is not
- uncertainty is hidden
- provenance is hidden
- failures are hidden
- user control is unclear
- the model boundary is not named

Pass only if:

- users can inspect why something happened
- users can recover from failure
- users can approve or reject high-stakes actions
- generated output is distinguished from verified state

## Source Gate

Reject if:

- references are only named, not analyzed
- source type is unclear
- no transferable pattern is extracted
- the source is a vibe screenshot
- the source is out of date for a changing topic

Pass only if:

- sources are classified as doctrine, evidence, inspiration, or noise
- at least one concrete pattern is extracted
- at least one anti-pattern is extracted
- implementation is checked against the extracted pattern
